# Critical Failure

## Project Overview

This project focuses on identifying products that represent critical business failures by combining pricing, customer satisfaction, financial risk, and sales performance metrics. The analysis helps uncover products that are overpriced, poorly rated, financially risky, and underperforming in sales, enabling data-driven corrective actions.

## Objective

To identify products that satisfy all of the following conditions:

* Product price is higher than its category average.
* Product rating is lower than its category average.
* Risk Score belongs to the top 20% of products.
* Sales performance is below the category median.

These products are classified as **Critical Failure Products** because they negatively impact profitability, customer satisfaction, and brand trust.

## Methodology

### Price Evaluation

Products were compared against category-level average prices to identify overpriced products.

### Customer Satisfaction Analysis

Product ratings were benchmarked against category average ratings.

### Risk Assessment

Risk Scores were used to identify financially vulnerable products.

### Sales Performance Analysis

Products with sales below the category median were flagged as underperformers.

### Critical Failure Identification

Products meeting all four conditions were classified as Critical Failure Products.

## Key Findings

* Multiple products were priced above category averages while receiving below-average ratings.
* High-risk products frequently demonstrated weak sales performance.
* A small group of products contributed disproportionately to business risk and customer dissatisfaction.
* These products represented the most urgent opportunities for business intervention.

## Why These Products Are Critical Business Failures

Products identified as Critical Failures create several business challenges:

* Customers perceive them as overpriced.
* Poor ratings reduce customer confidence and conversion rates.
* Weak sales reduce revenue generation.
* High risk exposure threatens profitability.

Without corrective action, these products can negatively affect both financial performance and brand reputation.

## Business Insights

The analysis enables managers to focus on products requiring immediate attention. Recommended actions include:

* Re-pricing products that exceed customer value expectations.
* Improving product quality and customer experience.
* Optimizing inventory allocation.
* Removing persistently underperforming products.

This targeted approach helps improve profitability, customer satisfaction, and operational efficiency.

## Conclusion

Pricing vs Satisfaction Exception Detection provides a structured framework for identifying products that simultaneously suffer from pricing, quality, risk, and sales issues. By addressing these critical failures early, businesses can reduce losses, improve customer trust, and strengthen overall portfolio performance.
